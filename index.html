Insertion Sort:



Insertion sort builds the sorted array by iteratively inserting each element into its correct position among the already sorted elements. Time complexity: O(n^2) in the worst case, but it can be efficient for small datasets or nearly sorted arrays.


Merge Sort:



Merge sort follows a divide-and-conquer approach. It divides the array into smaller subarrays, sorts them recursively, and then merges them back together to form the sorted array. Time complexity: O(n log n) in all cases, making it efficient for large datasets.


Quick Sort:



Quick sort also uses a divide-and-conquer strategy. It selects a pivot element, partitions the array into elements less than the pivot and elements greater than the pivot, and then recursively sorts the two partitions. Time complexity: O(n log n) on average and O(n^2) in the worst case, but it's often faster than other O(n log n) algorithms in practice.
